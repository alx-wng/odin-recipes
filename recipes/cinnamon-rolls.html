Cinnamon Rolls

Description

Make some extra-fluffy cinnamon rolls with cream cheese frosting!

Recipe adapted from bon appetit

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 1/4 cups whole milk
  • 5 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 1/4 oz instant yeast
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1/2 cup room temperature unsalted butter, cut into pieces

Filling

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup dark brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tbsp heavy cream
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Icing

  • 4 tbsp room temperature unsalted butter
  • all purpose flour (for surface)
  • 8 oz room temperature cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tbsp heavy cream
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Preparation

    Dough

  1. Whisk 3/4 cups of whole milk with 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour in saucepan until smooth. Place saucepan over medium heat and cook, whisking constantly, until mixture is thickened and whisk lines are visible on bottom of pan, about 2 minutes. Scrape tangzhong into the bowl of a stand mixer and let cool.
  2. Add 1 large egg, 1 1/4 oz instant yeast, 1/3 cup of granulated sugar, 1 tbsp salt, and remaining 1 1/2 cups whole milk to tangzhong and whisk to combine. Add remaining 5 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour. Fit bowl onto stand mixer; fit mixer with dough hook attachment. Mix on low speed until combined, about 1 minute. Increase speed to medium-low and mix, scraping down sides of bowl occasionally, until dough is smooth and elastic, 18-24 minutes.Pinch off a golf-ball-size piece of dough and stretch between your fingers as thin as possible without breaking. If you can see light through the dough, it's ready. If not, continue mixing a few more minutes, then try again.
  3. Add 1/2 cup room temperature unsalted butter, cut into pieces, to dough and mix on medium speed until incorporated, about 2 minutes. Continue mixing until dough pulls away cleanly from sides of bowl and is no longer tacky to the touch, 5-7 minutes longer. Remove bowl from mixer and cover with a damp kitchen towel. Let sit in a warm spot until doubled in volume, 60-90 minutes.

    4. Filling

  4. While the dough is rising, cook 1/2 cup unsalted butter in a small saucepan over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until butter foams and milk solids turn dark amber, about 4 minutes. Transfer to a heatproof bowl and let cool slightly. Add 1 cup dark brown sugar, 2 tbsp ground cinnamon, 2 tbsp heavy cream, and 1/2 tsp salt to butter and mix to combine.

    5. Icing and Assembly

  5. Butter two 9-inch cake pans. Punch down dough and transfer to a lightly floured surface. Stretch dough into a rough rectangle; if dough feels stiff, let sit 5-10 minutes to allow gluten to relax. Arrange dough with short side facing you and roll out to a 24 x 13 inch rectangle. Spread filling over dough in an even layer.
  6. Starting at edge closest to you, tightly roll up dough into a log; roll log seam side down. Using a sharp serrated knife and long sawing motions, trim ends of log; discard. Using serrated knife or, preferably, unflavored dental floss, cut log in half crosswise, then cut each half into 6 equal rounds. (If using dental floss, slide floss underneath dough, then cross over top and pull ends through.) Nestle 6 rounds, cut side down, in each prepared cake pan. Cover with damp kitchen towels and let sit in a warm spot until doubled in volume, about 30-50 minutes.
  7. Place a rack in middle of oven; preheat to 350Â°. Bake rolls until golden on top and puffed (don't be alarmed if they rise dramatically in the center), 25-30 minutes.
  8. Meanwhile, using an electric mixer on medium speed or a whisk, beat 4 tbsp room temperature unsalted butter, and 8 oz room temperature cream cheese, in a large bowl, scraping down sides of bowl as needed, until combined. Add 1/2 cups powdered sugar, 1 tbsp heavy cream, 1 tsp lemon zest, and 1/4 tsp salt and beat until mixture is smooth and sugar is incorporated. Taste and beat in more powdered sugar if desired. Thin with more cream as needed, a teaspoonful at a time, until frosting is thick but spreadable.
  9. Let cinnamon rolls cool in pan 5 minutes; spread frosting over.
